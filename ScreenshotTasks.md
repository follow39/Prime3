## Quick Tips for Screenshot Tasks

### Good Task Titles Should Be:
- **Specific**: "Complete quarterly report" not "Do work stuff"
- **Action-oriented**: Start with verbs (Complete, Finish, Write, Call, etc.)
- **Realistic**: Things people actually need to do
- **Relatable**: Your audience should see themselves in these tasks

### Good Descriptions Should:
- **Add context**: Why this task matters
- **Show constraints**: Deadlines, time estimates, dependencies
- **Be personal**: Include small details that make it feel real
- **Demonstrate value**: Show the app works for meaningful work

### Visual Variety:
Mix task lengths to show the UI handles both short and long content gracefully.

### Emotional Connection:
Include tasks that make viewers think "Yes! I need to do that too!" This creates instant identification with the app.

## Task Creation Guidelines

When creating your own screenshot tasks:

1. **Know Your Audience**: Professional? Student? Entrepreneur? Match tasks to target user
2. **Show Diversity**: Mix work and personal goals
3. **Be Authentic**: Real challenges people face, not corporate speak
4. **Add Personality**: Small details make tasks feel genuine
5. **Demonstrate Value**: Show the app helps with meaningful work, not trivial errands
6. **Test Readability**: Tasks should be scannable at screenshot size
7. **Cultural Sensitivity**: Avoid tasks that might not translate across cultures
8. **Aspirational But Achievable**: Goals that inspire without intimidating

## A/B Testing Suggestions

Consider creating two versions and testing:

### Version A: Professional Focus
- More work-related tasks
- Business-oriented language
- Career advancement themes

### Version B: Life Balance Focus
- Equal work/personal split
- Wellness and relationship tasks
- Sustainable living themes

Track which version resonates better with your target audience and optimize accordingly.
